Project Statement

Material Reclamation & Urban Healing

THEREFORM Offices reimagines the workplace as a site for urban healing. The project challenges conventional new construction by prioritizing salvaged materials — reclaimed metals, aluminum cans, construction waste — treated with an oxidizing agent to create a weathered copper aesthetic that becomes a record of transformation.

Located in downtown Las Vegas, the design connects to the urban context while creating a new model for sustainable office development. The building demonstrates how reclaimed materials can achieve both aesthetic excellence and environmental responsibility.

Structure

The Salvaged Skeleton

The structural approach utilizes a salvaged skeleton — reclaimed steel and timber from deconstructed buildings in the Las Vegas area. A truss grid structure assembled from reclaimed steel creates a repetitive, geometric ordering system that defines the building's depth and scale. This approach significantly reduces embodied carbon while celebrating the history of materials with previous lives.

Interior

The Living Stitch

A central planted spine runs vertically through all floors, creating a landscape of circulation. Reused metal framing forms structure and shade. Natural light floods down from above, animating the vegetation and blurring the boundary between office and garden.

Interior

The Working Yard

Reuse continues to shape the everyday workspace. Crushed cans become partitions, wood pallets from casino deliveries are refinished into tables, and vinyl scraps with packing peanuts form transparent beanbags. Reclaimed wood lines the ceiling and bottles hang as improvised fixtures. The project turns waste streams into a functional and expressive interior landscape.
