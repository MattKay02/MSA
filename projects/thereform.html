Project Statement

THEREFORM Offices is a studio project exploring the potential of material reclamation in commercial architecture. The project challenges the conventional approach to new construction by prioritizing salvaged materials and adaptive reuse strategies.

Located in downtown Las Vegas, the design connects to the urban context while creating a new model for sustainable office development. The building demonstrates how reclaimed materials can achieve both aesthetic excellence and environmental responsibility.

Design Concepts

The project is organized around two key concepts: "Connecting Corners" — which creates visual and physical links between the building and its urban surroundings — and "Shifting Levels" — which introduces dynamic spatial relationships between work areas.

Salvaged Skeleton

The structural approach utilizes a "salvaged skeleton" — reclaimed steel and timber from deconstructed buildings in the Las Vegas area. This approach significantly reduces the project's embodied carbon while celebrating the history of materials with previous lives.

Interior Environments

Interior spaces like "The Living Stitch" and "Working Yard" redefine the conventional office environment. These spaces blur the boundaries between work and wellness, incorporating biophilic elements and flexible configurations that respond to the changing nature of work.

Sustainability Approach

Beyond material reclamation, the project incorporates passive cooling strategies, rainwater harvesting, and on-site renewable energy generation. The building aims to demonstrate that commercial architecture can be both economically viable and environmentally regenerative.
