Project Statement

Ecological Sanctuary

In a world of rising temperatures, increasing air pollution, and the impending collapse of ecosystems, new restrictions and rules are imposed. OVA looks to become a sanctuary for natural regeneration, education, and freedom — providing a space where children can thrive within the future ecological boundaries of Las Vegas.

OVA Kindergarten places a strong emphasis on community engagement, providing spaces that can be used by the public during non-school hours, creating a flexible and welcoming environment. This flexibility is continued throughout the project, adequately preparing for future challenges.

Context

The Site

Located in Spring Valley, the site holds untapped potential for creating an inward ecological oasis — a place where the tangible natural world can be brought directly into the daily experiences of young learners.

Design Move

Flexibility

The classroom ribbon is designed around a principle of radical flexibility. Rooms open, merge, and reconfigure to support different types of learning — from individual focus to large group collaboration. No fixed arrangement is assumed.

Flexibility diagrams — classroom ribbon configurations
Design Move

Root Tables

Adaptive storage solutions inspired by desert plant root systems. Sensor-activated safety mechanisms allow furniture to transform between configurations, supporting different age groups and learning modes throughout the day.

Material

Weathered Copper

Vertical copper fins wrap the exterior, creating ever-changing light patterns throughout the day while providing necessary shade in the desert climate. Over time, the copper develops a natural green patina — a living record of the building's age and the desert environment's weathering.
