Project Statement

VOID//ED explores the tension between human intervention in nature and nature's inevitable reclamation. It frames meditation as a subjective and evolving experience, unfolding over decades.

The project invites introspection while bearing witness to the larger struggle between humanity and the natural world. It offers both solitude and refuge from the harsh landscape while simultaneously embracing its vastness and hostility, challenging visitors to reflect on resilience, impermanence, and the potential for a more harmonious, sustainable coexistence.

Void filter detail

VOID//ED employs a sand catcher design, inviting nature to intrude upon the space and gradually reclaim it.

This continual encroachment reinforces the ephemeral nature of the structure, blurring the line between the built and the organic, and reminding visitors of the impermanence of human intervention.

"Stone and sand whisper the same truth: What we build is only ever borrowed."
— Carlos Scarpa
1 Month The void begins as a purely man-made intervention, sharply contrasting the natural and artificial landscapes.
Sand gradually filters into the space, subtly softening the boundary between architecture and nature.
10 Years The void is all but consumed, forcing visitors to scale the dunes to reach the main cavern. Nature is reclaiming its ground.
Half of the structure is engulfed, compelling visitors to sit on the dunes and fostering a sense of harmony with nature.
20 Years The void is now filled, restoring the natural balance, while the tunnel and oculus remain as a lingering reminder of human impermanence.
