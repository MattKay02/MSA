Project Statement

VOID//ED explores the tension between human intervention in nature and nature's inevitable reclamation. It frames meditation as a subjective and evolving experience, unfolding over decades.

The project invites introspection while bearing witness to the larger struggle between humanity and the natural world. It offers both solitude and refuge from the harsh landscape while simultaneously embracing its vastness and hostility, challenging visitors to reflect on resilience, impermanence, and the potential for a more harmonious, sustainable coexistence.

Temporal Progression

The design acknowledges the passage of time as a fundamental element of the meditation experience. The space transforms over decades, offering visitors entirely different encounters depending on when they arrive.

Sand Catcher Design

VOID//ED employs a sand catcher design, inviting nature to intrude upon the space and gradually reclaim it. This continual encroachment reinforces the ephemeral nature of the structure, blurring the line between the built and the organic.

Evolution of Space

The void begins as a purely man-made intervention, sharply contrasting the natural and artificial landscapes. Over time, sand gradually filters into the space, subtly softening the boundary between architecture and nature.
